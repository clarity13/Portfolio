Haste iOS App Design

The Haste app innovates on grocery shopping by bringing the ease of online shopping to stores all in the palm of your hands

View prototype

The Problem

Grocery shopping has generally three types of people in the world. Shoppers who are impatient, shoppering who use coupons to save money, and the third shopper who doesn’t really care. I know that most of the time people are the third shopper because it is always great to save time and money. During my research I didn’t find many applications that make shopping easier except Sams Club. The more advanced version of this application is Amazons grocery store which is extremely technology based. Shopping in stores need to get with the times and needs to innovate. As of right now it isn’t as seamless as it could be.

Users & Audience

The target users for this application is anyone who needs to shop for food. Mostly families, and people living on their own.

Design Process

I decided to follow IDEO’s Human-centered Design. Also implemented Lean UX Design Thinking to make sure that my design decisions were supported by user research and feedback

This application became an idea after realizing that shopping has been the same for awhile and with technology growing it can be improved and made easier for everyone. I began looking at how other stores had their applications set up like Target, Sams Club, etc. I began observing my daily activities as I shopped and the type of people who were in these stores.

User Analysis

Surveys were sent out to get an idea of what type of users would most likely use this product and what their habits are. According to the results it became aware to me that not many users took advantage of coupons which in my case I do not also sometimes myself. It is way too much work to always be carrying around coupons. I took note of this as well as trying to figure out if people are tech savvy. A lot of people in super markets are also baby boomers and some or not that great with technology. Yes they will have smart phones but I take into account that I will be having older users. Knowing that I planned on making the layout of the application as simple as possible.

Competitive Analysis

Many of the competitors had their own strengths but were a little lack luster. Grocery Pal had the prices of some of the items but it didn’t feel complete as they didn’t have specific brands. Cartwheel is a Target only app which only has coupons. Big Oven is an application that helps you find recipes which is very useful for someone who loves to try to cook new things. Breaking down all of their strengths and weaknesses will give an idea on how Haste will stand up to the competition and go above and beyond their limitations.

User Flows

With all apps, I wanted to make it as seamless as possible. With that Figuring out how a user would use the application came down to researching how other great applications worked. The most important user flows were the actual sign up process because if it was complicated it would deter many users from actually even trying to use Haste. The two other important aspects of the application were the way you add items into the cart which is baed off scanning items with your phones camera. Third is the checkout which had to show everything that you are buying so that you know what you are paying for. Once you are satisfied with what you see you may pay with a push of a button.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Starting the process of the low fidelity mock ups on Sketch made everything easier to know how much information can fit on a specific screen. I wanted to give the users of this application a way to make their experience as fluid as possible. With that in mind the actual cart would have pictures in place so that you know exactly what you are buying. I believe the most important part of this is to know where your money is going so I decided to implement reciepts for all past purchases.

Homepage 3

I realized that my first iteration of the homepage was simple and to the point but it seemed to be missing some personality to it. That being said the other two versions is when I added a recipes page to further grab the retention of users. I personally thought that Homepage 2 had a nice appeal but after multiple testings it turned out homepage 3 was liked by 63% of users. After taking a step back I didn’t understand the appeal but with further research I did make a mistake of thinking homepage 2 was the best fit.

Having multiple layouts whether it is obvious or not that one is better than the other is irrelevant. Having options always helps and brings out which design works better. In this case layout 3 was preferred with 88% of users because of the visual hierarchy. Doing this, solidifed which layout I was going to continue with through testing.

Button Quantity 1

Button Quantity 2

Buttons are a huge part of an app being user friendly. If they are too small then you have a terrible experience. The preference here is scttered but I saw myself gravitating towards the circular buttons as it visually fit better. The only issue that it was a little small for people who had smaller phones. Knowing this information and designing for everyone, I made a mistake thinking everyone had similar phone sizes and I adapted to the smaller screens.

Style Guide

Colors were created in a way for the user to feel welcomed so that when the app is opened they would want to engage with it. The colors have fun tones but should not be used all over the place because it would make it a little overwhelming.
